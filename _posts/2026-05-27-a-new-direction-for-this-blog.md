I have not been posting for the past couple months, not just that, I am not even keeping a journal anymore, I understand the importance of having a written record for acquiring a sense of flow of time. Journalling is what prevents us from thinking that time just flew by as we then have the means to look past and see what we did in all that time.

But to journal for myself no longer feels important, and unless I am working on a project the urge to think up an idea to write about is just not there, as for improving writing skills goes, I feel like it would be better to acquire the skill of speaking in new languages that try to hone some artistic ability to weave words together that work for some but not others.

In the mean time I have been exposed to new blog structures and rss feeds that has made me interesting to try out something new. For the past few weeks I have been following javascriptweekly which has an rss feed sharing major releases and discussions surrounding the community. It feels more handpicked than wasting hours at hackernews, and thus I believe I should turn my blog around in a similar structure, but rather than focussing on just a limited topic I plan to turn this blog as my personal bookmark and home for opinionated remarks on things I encounter, rather than a daily thing with date and time logs my weekly journal focusses on new and cool things I found, and links to things I'd lose otherwise.

The posts from here on would probably be a lot longer on here. I also plan to go back and add together some old posts that are too similar in scope.
